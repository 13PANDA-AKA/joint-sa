After 60, Everyday Movement Started Feeling Different — Here’s What One Cape Town Resident Did

Cape Town. John M. says the changes were gradual. Longer mornings. Shorter walks. More effort on stairs.

“It didn’t happen overnight,” he explains. “It slowly became harder to move the way I used to.”

Mobility decline often starts subtly — many people wait years before exploring supportive options.

Why Movement Changes With Time

Collagen production decreases naturally. Flexibility may shift. Comfort levels can change slowly over time.

Instead of ignoring early signs, more South Africans are now looking into proactive mobility support.

Quick Self‑Assessment

✔ Morning stiffness lasting longer than before
✔ Reduced flexibility compared to previous years
✔ Needing more effort for everyday movement

If you relate to at least one of the above, reviewing supportive joint options may be worth considering.
